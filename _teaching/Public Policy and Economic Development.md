This course analyzes the impact of public policy on economic development, especially the allocation of resources and the distribution of income in the economy. 
By learning this course, students will know how to use the tools of microeconomics and empirical analysis (empirical methods are the priority) to address the following questions: When should the government intervene in the economy? How might the government intervene? And what are the effects of those interventions on economic outcomes? Main topics include state, government, bureaucracy, education, etc. 
This is a Ph.D. level course. In addition to learning the basic knowledge of public policy and economics, we are going to review important literature in this field, especially focusing on empirical studies in the context of China and other developing countries.
